What is housing like?

What is housing like?

Great places to live and make friends: That’s the UC Davis housing experience for undergraduates, and graduate and professional students. Here is where you find the support to establish your college community. A big bonus for Davis students is that we’ve got high-quality food options on-and off-campus. Find answers to some questions that you might have about living at UC Davis. For more, visit Student Housing’s FAQ.

What are my first-year choices?

Freshmen and transfers live in a variety of residence halls that include Tercero 3, our first to earn the top national rating for being sustainable. If you are concerned about liking your potential roommate, did you know you can request a roommate at UC Davis? Learn more about your resources available through Student Housing.

What if I’m not a freshman or transfer?

After your first-year undergraduate year at UC Davis, including if you are a graduate or professional student, the options expand. Student Housing operates many housing areas. There’s also West Village, the nation’s largest planned zero net energy community. Private apartments and homes are also available in our student-friendly Davis community.

What about a car?

How much does it cost for first-years on campus?

Learn more about our housing rates and costs for the dining commons meal plans on our housing website’s fees page.

Freshmen living on campus aren’t allowed to bring their cars or purchase a campus parking permit. But we have a great student-operated Unitrans bus service free for UC Davis undergraduate students that serves the entire city of Davis. Most students either bicycle or walk to and from campus. Check out the campus parking program for residents or parking information for those living off campus.

How’s the food?

Our award-winning UC Davis residential dining commons offer a delicious array of choices to suit all tastes, and the UC Davis campus and downtown are known for tasty fare that doesn’t bust the budget. Beyond the dining program in the residential halls, students can find eateries across campus including those at the Silo, Coffee House and Student Community Center. Or you can get a quick bite at one of our many food trucks. Often students use Aggie Cash as an easy way to pay for campus food. It’s easy: You can either load money onto a card online or pay for it at our Meal Card Office.

In addition to all of the dining options on campus, the city of Davis has a wide variety of restaurants with reasonable prices and flexible hours perfect for students. Some of them even accept Aggie Cash!
